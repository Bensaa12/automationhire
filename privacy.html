This Privacy Policy explains how AutomationHire.co.uk ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, stores and protects your personal data. We are committed to complying with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. Please read this policy carefully before using our website or services.

1. Who We Are

AutomationHire.co.uk is an online directory and marketplace connecting businesses with AI automation agencies and freelancers. We are the data controller for personal data collected through this website.

Our Details

  • Website: automationhire.co.uk
  • Contact email: privacy@automationhire.co.uk
  • Registered in: England and Wales

If you have any questions about how we handle your personal data, please contact us at the email address above before raising a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

2. Data We Collect

We collect different data depending on how you use our website. We only collect what is necessary for the purpose stated.

2.1 — Visitors (Browsing the Directory)

  • IP address and approximate location (via server logs)
  • Browser type, device type, operating system
  • Pages visited, time spent, referral source
  • Search queries entered on the directory

This data is collected automatically and is used only for analytics and security purposes. It is not used to identify you personally.

2.2 — Clients (Requesting Quotes or Getting Matched)

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Company name (optional)
  • Phone number (optional)
  • Project description, budget and timeline
  • Tools and platforms you currently use

2.3 — Providers (Listing Your Business)

  • Full name and business name
  • Email address and password (encrypted)
  • Phone number (optional)
  • Business description, services, tools and industries
  • Website URL and LinkedIn profile
  • Location (city and country)
  • Pricing information
  • Portfolio content and case studies
  • Certifications uploaded
  • Billing information (processed by Stripe — we do not store card details)

2.4 — Newsletter Subscribers

  • Email address
  • Date of subscription and source page

2.5 — Reviews

  • Reviewer name, company and role
  • Email address (not published publicly)
  • Review text and star rating

In the event of a merger, acquisition or sale of all or part of our business, your data may be transferred to the acquiring entity. You will be notified by email of any such change and your rights under this policy will be maintained.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary for the purpose it was collected, or as required by law.

Data Type Retention Period
Provider account and listing data Duration of account + 2 years after account deletion
Lead request data 3 years from submission date
Payment and billing records 7 years (required by UK tax law)
Newsletter subscriber email Until you unsubscribe or request deletion
Reviews Until you request removal or the provider listing is deleted
Server logs and analytics 90 days
Support correspondence 2 years from last interaction

When data reaches the end of its retention period, it is securely deleted or anonymised so it can no longer be linked to an individual.

7. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data. To exercise any of them, contact us at privacy@automationhire.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days.

Right of Access

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (commonly called a Subject Access Request or SAR). We will provide this free of charge in a commonly used electronic format.

Right to Rectification

If any personal data we hold about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to have it corrected. Providers can update most data directly from their dashboard.

Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data where there is no compelling reason for us to continue processing it. Note that we may need to retain some data for legal or contractual reasons (e.g. payment records for 7 years).

Right to Restrict Processing

You can ask us to restrict how we use your data while a dispute is being resolved — for example if you contest the accuracy of your data or have objected to processing.

Right to Data Portability

You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format (e.g. JSON or CSV) and to transmit it to another service.

Right to Object

Where we rely on legitimate interests as our legal basis, you have the right to object to processing. We will stop unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on your consent (e.g. newsletter), you can withdraw consent at any time by clicking "Unsubscribe" in any email or contacting us directly. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe we have not handled your data properly, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

  • Website: ico.org.uk
  • Phone: 0303 123 1113
  • Post: ICO, Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire, SK9 5AF

We do ask that you contact us first so we have the opportunity to resolve your concern directly.

8. Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. We use the following types:

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
Essential Required for the site to function — authentication sessions, security tokens Session / 7 days
Functional Remember your preferences (e.g. filter settings, billing toggle) 30 days
Analytics Understand how visitors use the site (page views, traffic sources). We use privacy-first analytics that do not track individuals across sites. 90 days
Stripe Fraud prevention and payment processing on checkout pages Session / 1 year

We do not use advertising or tracking cookies. We do not share cookie data with social media platforms for advertising purposes.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling essential cookies will prevent you from logging in or using interactive features. You can also use a browser extension such as uBlock Origin to block non-essential cookies.

9. International Data Transfers

Our primary infrastructure is located within the European Economic Area (EEA) or in countries that the UK has deemed to provide adequate data protection. Specifically:

  • Supabase — AWS EU (Ireland)
  • Vercel — Edge network with data processing in the EU where selected
  • Stripe — Operates under EU–US Data Privacy Framework and UK adequacy decision
  • Resend — EU infrastructure

Where any transfer occurs to a country without a UK adequacy decision, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place such as UK International Data Transfer Agreements (IDTAs) or Standard Contractual Clauses.

10. Children's Privacy

AutomationHire is a business-to-business service intended for adults aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under the age of 13. If you believe a child has submitted personal data to us, please contact us immediately at privacy@automationhire.co.uk and we will delete it promptly.

11. Security

We take the security of your personal data seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures including:

  • All data in transit encrypted via TLS 1.2+ (HTTPS)
  • All data at rest encrypted using AES-256 encryption
  • Passwords stored as bcrypt hashes — we cannot see your password
  • Row-level security (RLS) policies ensure providers can only access their own data
  • Payment card data handled exclusively by Stripe — we never touch it
  • API keys and secrets stored as environment variables, never in code
  • Access to the production database limited to essential personnel only

Despite these measures, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. If you discover a security vulnerability, please report it responsibly to security@automationhire.co.uk.

Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the ICO within 72 hours of becoming aware of it, and notify affected individuals without undue delay as required under UK GDPR Article 34.
